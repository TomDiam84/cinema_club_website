About us


Our objective


The objective of the Cinema Section is to promote cinematographic education among students in order to make them knowledgeable film goers. Moreover, it gives them the opportunity to create so as to produce films which will socially elevate them as well as the Academic Community to which they belong. These days, the Cinema Section has a full weekly programme of seminars (aesthetics-technique analysis-direction-cinematography, animation), screening of 35mm films and a video projector, which is improved and enriched on a daily basis, the seminars as conducted by older members of the Cinema Society, who voluntarily and with a lot of enthusiasm prepare a series of courses which apart from familiarizing students with the area of knowledge, motivate them to think,analyse and discuss.Attending screenings, workshops and seminars is for free. Furthermore, this Section supports its members in producing 8 mm and 16mm short films and digital video, by providing technical material and cinematographic equipment. There are also events, such as festivals and dedications.


Our place


Screenings and seminars take place on the ground floor of the University Club, inside Iris Film Theatre (15 Ippokratous & Akadimias Str.) in the centre of Athens (map). The following photos show the Iris cinema hall.


Iris is the most historic cinema of Athens. It has been in the heart of the city since 1930, on the ground floor of the University Club, a listed building that is a model of the modern Greek architecture of the interwar period, designed by Alexandros Nikoloudis. The building is a legacy on behalf of Antonis Papadakis with the clear term that its use focuses on the moral and material support of students, whether it concerns their needs (feeding, healthcare) or their intellectual cultivation and support for their cultural activities (theater, cinema etc.). The space has a parallel course with the Athens University Cultural Club (POFPA), under its management, which is made up of Theater, Cinema, Photographic and Dance, from which many thousands of students have spent - and not only - all these years.

F.A.Q.


  • What is the Cinema Section?

    The Cinema Section of the A.U.C.C.S. is a volunteer student art club of the University of Athens, which holds film seminars, screenings and other educational and cultural activities, bearing the primary responsibility and obligation to operate the Iris cinema theater on the ground floor of the University Club.

  • What is the A.U.C.C.S.?

    The Athens University Club Cultural Society is the central cultural body of the students of the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens, which consists of four artistic fields (Cinematography, Photographic, Theatrical, and Dancing) and is housed in the University Club (Ippokratous 15 th Academy). More information: faq

  • What are the core activities?

    The Cinematographic Section has a full weekly seminar program (script-technical-analysis-directing) and video-film-based educational projections. The seminars are conducted by older and younger members of the Cinematographic Sector, who volunteerly attend a series of meetings that, besides familiarizing themselves with the subject, offer the occasion for reflection and discussion.

    In addition, the Section supports, by providing technical materials and cinematic equipment, the production of short films of its members in digital format, as well as 8 mm film. Finally, it organizes events such as festivals and tributes.

  • Where do the seminars take place?

    The cinematographic sector is mainly active at the Iris cinema, located at the ground floor of the University Club (Ippokratous 15 & Akadimias), and has its offices and workshops on the mezzanine of the Club (Room 16), as well as a room in the Kostis Palamas building (Pink Building - Akadimias 48 & Sina).

  • Target Audience?

    The participation and access to the cultural activities of the Cinematographic Sector is free for all, students and filmmakers, based on the principles of operation of the Department, but also in the general framework of educational activities of the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens. The registration of the participants as members of the Sector constitutes a basic axis of active support for the space and the educational and creative effort that takes place in it.



  • Basic principles

    The basic principles of the cinematographic industry are equality between members - both collective and individual, the non-profit character of the activities, the collective and democratic processes, and finally the unrestricted artistic pursuit of its members - in the context of cinematographic art.

  • Goals
    • Providing interested students and filmmakers, free access and participation in cinematographic art activities.
    • The promotion of the University as an active cultural player in the cinematic activities of our country and as a helper of the efforts of young artists and creators.
    • The liberalization of artistic expression and the promotion of artistic creation in every aspect of the cultural space.
    • The aesthetic cultivation and the development of the personality and social participation of the members.
  • Possibilities of artistic expression

    In the framework of the practical / technical seminars, film exercises are held to familiarize the members of the Sector with the cinematographic subject, as well as equipment that is available. At a later stage, our members have the knowledge and experience to implement their own design productions. There is also the possibility to view cinematic content articles from Bobina magazine, either in print or on a web-based version.



  • How do I join a membership?

    Membership is made by completing a simple application, regular registration days at the beginning of the academic year, or during the year in the hours of the activities, after consultation with the seminar managers.

  • Is there a participation fee?

    Participation in cinematographic activities is free for all, within the framework of the operating principles of the Department, however, it is optional to be eligible to participate in the extraordinary logistical needs of the sector during the registrations.

  • Do I need to know anything about my participation in the seminars?

    Participation in screenings and seminars is free and welcome for all, but on the basis of basic requirements, in order not to impede the smooth running of the activities

  • Can I get my own movie?

    It is possible for those members of our field who have acquired theoretical and technical knowledge through the seminars to make short films, either in the form of exercises or in the form of their own initiative, within the framework of the productions of the Cinematographic Sector.

  • Can I participate more actively?

    In addition to participating in theoretical and technical seminars, we are able to further express our artistic expression through the creation of short films, authoring of cinematic content (Bobina magazine), as well as organizationally, by participating in collective decisions, co-organizing seminars, even graphically, in the projection of the Sector work. Of course and with any other proposal, which is in line with the principles and purposes of the sector.

  • Resources

    The resources of the Department come from the University Club of the University of Athens as well as donations by members and third parties.
